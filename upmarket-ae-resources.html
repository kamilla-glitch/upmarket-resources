How PM Services Pricing & Scoping Works

All AU Project Management services are priced and scoped exclusively by the AU PM team. PM services are bespoke — pricing is based on deal complexity, number of locations, technical requirements, and the level of support required. To receive a quote, engage the AU PM team via a DSR as early in the deal cycle as possible. The PM assigned to your deal will assess the scope and produce a custom Statement of Work (SOW) outlining services and pricing for inclusion in the contract.

🚫 No deal should be quoted for PM services without a quote coming directly from the AU PM team.
🚫 No Scope of Work (SOW) is to be defined, authored, or modified by anyone other than the AU PM team.
✅ Discounting can be discussed with your direct lead — no PM leadership approval required.

Who We Are

Meet the AU Project Management Team team

The AU Project Management team is a dedicated group of Project Managers embedded within the Global Seller Onboarding team. We specialise in managing complex, high-value upmarket implementations from pre-sale all the way through to post-go-live handoff. Think of us as your implementation partner — we work alongside you and your seller to scope, plan, and deliver projects that a standard CSM or AM engagement simply isn't built to handle. We're a small, focused team, which means when you bring us in, you get genuine expertise and dedicated attention on your deal.

How We Right-Size Your Implementation Support

Three tiers of AU PM team engagement based on deal complexity and seller profile

Not all upmarket deals require the same level of PM involvement. The AU PM team scales its engagement to match the complexity and strategic importance of each deal — from a light-touch model for simpler implementations right through to white-glove, full-service support for lighthouse sellers. Understanding which tier fits your deal helps you set the right expectations with the seller from day one and ensures the AU PM team is deployed where it creates the most impact.

👍 Half — Light Touch
SMB/mid-market sellers, SFF & Payments, simple AM use-cases (e.g. SPOS → RST migrations). Examples: Tyson Foods, Andersen Bakery, Hometown
• No pre-sales engagement
• Light-touch, key stakeholders only
• Emphasise time-to-go-live, short timeline
• No weekly syncs — essential calls only
• Delegate tasks to Global Seller Onboarding Team, BPO, or 3P
• High-level project plan for timeline alignment
• Implementation pricing/quote as-needed
• AM handoff for sellers qualifying at $1M+ GPV
• Enable sellers to self-serve wherever possible
👏 Existing — Standard Upmarket
Standard upmarket accounts. Examples: CFA Corp, Raw Juice, Northstar Restaurant Group
• High-touch engagement
• Weekly syncs
• Detailed, maintained project plan
• SOW included in contract
• Cross-functional coordination (SAMPS, SE, TAS, AM)
• Enable sellers to own config after initial setup
• Project manage 3P technicians
• Drive timeline, mitigate risks of delays
🌟 Double — Lighthouse
Lighthouse/strategic sellers. Examples: Bluestone Lane, Steak n' Shake, Katz's Deli, Sweetgreen
• White-glove, maximum-touch engagement
• Weekly or twice-weekly syncs
• SOW included in contract
• Robust, constantly updated project plan
• Full cross-functional coordination (SAMPS, SE, TAS, AM)
• Early engagement of AM and TAS
• AU PM team owns all account configuration
• Seller-directed implementation support
• Oversee 3P technicians; "on call" during installations
• "Kitchen sink" support approach

Rules of Engagement — What the AU PM Team Owns

Clear scope boundaries across each phase of the deal cycle

The AU PM team's Rules of Engagement define exactly what falls within PM scope, what we support, and what sits outside our remit at each stage of the deal. These boundaries exist to prevent scope creep, enable clean handoffs, and ensure consistent delivery across all engagements. If you're unsure whether something falls to PM or another team, this is your reference point.

1
During the Sale Phase
Pre-sale through to contract close
✅Take ownership of maintaining the deal brief (AE initiates creation)
✅Partner with SAMPS to assess solution viability — product limitations, feature gaps, and implementation risks
✅Partner with AE, SAMPS, and SE to design and propose the recommended solution, including architecture and workarounds
✅Define and communicate the implementation approach — timelines, PM engagement model, onboarding expectations
✅Author Statements of Work (SOWs) covering scope, ownership, known gaps, and technical architecture
✅Participate in and travel for in-person pitch meetings when required
✅Advise AEs on pricing for PM services; coordinate 1P and 3P onsite installation quotes
✅Contribute to RFP responses related to product functionality and implementation
✅Define pilot parameters, duration, and success criteria
🚫Outside PM scope: Creating CPQ quotes · Sending hardware · Leading product demos · Negotiating SaaS, hardware, or service pricing
2
During Implementation
Post-close through to go-live
✅Create and maintain a detailed project plan; share updates with internal and external stakeholders
✅Manage issue escalation — triage, Jira/bug creation, issue tracking, and stakeholder communication
✅Own customer-facing comms around escalations and issue resolution; escalate internally to SE, Product, Eng as needed
✅Develop bespoke training curriculum; lead or delegate (to Global Seller Onboarding Team or 3P) training sessions including train-the-trainer
✅Create seller-facing documentation — training materials, configuration guides, one-pagers
✅Schedule and facilitate recurring stakeholder meetings; provide regular status updates and implementation recaps
✅File detailed feature requests to #sq-seller-feedback-intake; maintain feature gap and issue tracker
✅Coordinate with partners on Square-owned implementation requirements; plan deployments with installation partners
✅Dispatch and own communication with 3P vendors — scheduling, escalations, and feedback
✅Oversee franchisee/location deliverables for multi-LE deals where Global Seller Onboarding Team packages are sold
🚫Outside PM scope: Implementing partner solutions · Coordinating or executing hardware shipments via CPQ
3
Post-Implementation
Project close through to AM handoff
✅Obtain seller acceptance that implementation scope is complete to enable billing and AM handoff
✅Provide initial post-go-live support to ensure a successful transition
✅Apply billing templates (for non-CPQ, SFF sellers only)
✅Engage TAS for phased rollouts where post-live support is needed while implementation continues at other locations
✅Own completion of AM handoff documentation and updates to the deal brief; introduce seller to the inheriting AM
✅Conduct internal retrospectives with deal stakeholders when appropriate
🚫Outside PM scope: Following up on unpaid invoices · Updating CPT rates · Providing indefinite or ongoing seller support post-go-live

Best Practices for Working with the AU PM team

How to get the best results, by deal phase

The single biggest lever you have for a successful AU PM team engagement is timing — the earlier you bring us in, the better the outcome for you and your seller. The AU PM team is most effective when we're involved in shaping the deal, not just inheriting it. Here's what great partnership looks like at each phase of the deal cycle.

1
Early Sales Cycle
Pre-sale through to deal close
⏰Engage the AU PM team as early as possible. The sooner you bring us in, the more value we can add — we'll partner with you on solutioning, SOW creation, scoping, and pitching implementation services directly to the seller.
💬When in doubt, ping us. If you're unsure whether a deal warrants AU PM team involvement, just ask. A quick message is far better than waiting until it's too late.
🤝Engage SAMPS in tandem. The AU PM team and SAMPS work together seamlessly — we jointly scope solutions, determine product fit, and uncover feature gaps. Loop both teams in together wherever possible.
📋File a DSR with as much detail as possible. Less is not more here. Include full deal context, link relevant docs, attach call recordings, and provide anything that gives the AU PM team a complete picture from day one.
📞Sync with your AU PM rep before introducing them to the seller. Take 15 minutes to align on deal context, key stakeholders, and agreed next steps before any seller-facing introduction.
🛡️Once engaged, the AU PM team is your partner. You should feel confident handing off implementation responsibility — we'll keep you informed and involved throughout, but your deal is in good hands.
2
During Implementation
Post-close through to go-live
⚠️ $10M+ projects only — deals under this GPV floor will be low touch
📧Include your AU PM rep on all communications. Once the AU PM rep is involved, every relevant email and message should include them — this is non-negotiable and ensures continuity and proper documentation.
📅Weekly syncs are standard. The AU PM team schedules and leads recurring syncs with the seller. The TAE should be added to this cadence and is expected to join all calls. AU PM team provides written follow-up after every meeting.
📊A live project plan will be maintained throughout. The AU PM team creates and manages the project plan in ClickUp, circulates it to internal and external stakeholders, and updates it regularly. This is your single source of truth on deal progress.
🚨The AU PM team owns issue escalation. Any escalated issues are triaged by the AU PM team — we file Jiras and maintain an Issue Tracker. Route any seller-raised issues or blockers through your AU PM rep, not directly.
🎓Training is bespoke and AU PM team-led. The AU PM team customises onboarding to each seller's specific needs, leads train-the-trainer sessions, and creates seller-facing documentation tailored to their setup.
🏗️Onsites are AU PM team-led. TAEs should not be onsite for implementations or installations unless they have a very specific, pre-agreed role — discussed and aligned in advance with AU PM team, the 3P partner, and other relevant parties.

3P Vendor Communication — Do's & Don'ts

Clear rules of engagement when third-party vendors are involved

When a deal involves third-party technology vendors or installers, the rules around communication are strict — and for good reason. Bypassing the AU PM team on 3P comms creates documentation gaps, escalation failures, and performance coaching blind spots that are very difficult to recover from. Follow these guidelines closely.

✅ Do
✅ Route all 3P communication through the AU PM team
✅ Use SFDC or the designated Slack thread for all escalations and documentation
✅ Discuss any planned onsite involvement with the AU PM team in advance
✅ Keep the AU PM team copied on all seller and vendor communications
✅ Maintain visibility at all times — it's key for performance coaching and issue escalation
🚫 Don't
🚫 Schedule onsites directly with 3P tech without the AU PM team alignment
🚫 Communicate with 3P vendors directly or outside Square channels
🚫 Introduce sellers to 3P technicians or 3P leadership without the AU PM team involvement
🚫 Go onsite for implementations without a clearly defined, pre-agreed role
🚫 Take unilateral actions on a deal once the AU PM team is engaged — all decisions should be coordinated

Documentation Best Practices

Key documents the AU PM team owns and maintains throughout the deal cycle

Throughout the deal cycle the AU PM team is responsible for producing and maintaining a core set of documents. These aren't just internal records — they're the primary way knowledge is transferred between teams, and the foundation for clean AM handoffs. As an AE or AM, understanding what each document covers and who originates it will help you contribute the right information at the right time.

📝
Statement of Work (SOW) go/sow
Outlines implementation services and price, details task ownership across seller and Square, and highlights workflow changes. Sign-off from the seller is particularly important for SFF deals.
  • Originated and maintained by PM
  • Included in the contract
  • Sample SOWs: Bareburger, Cookie Plug
📊
Project Plan
Developed early in the deal cycle and maintained throughout. Tracks milestones, task ownership, location deployment, and implementation progress. Shared with internal and external stakeholders as the single source of truth.
  • Originated and maintained by PM in ClickUp
  • Examples: LiveNation, Steak Escape, Interdent
📋
Deal Brief go/dealbrief
A living document that evolves with the deal — containing deal context, solution details, commercials, key stakeholders, and a feature gap tracker. Originated by the AE, maintained by PM, handed off to AM and TAS.
  • AE initiates, PM maintains
  • Handed off to AM and TAS at project close
🤝
AM Handoff Doc go/amhandoff
Consolidated deal context to facilitate a warm handoff to AM. Included in the Deal Brief for complex deals; used standalone for lower-complexity engagements where a full Deal Brief isn't created.
  • Completed by PM; AE input required
  • Reviewed in internal handoff meeting with AE, AM, TAS

Communication Best Practices

How the AU PM team manages deal comms — and what's expected from you

Clear, well-structured communication is one of the most important things the AU PM team manages throughout an engagement. Every deal has a dedicated Slack channel, automatically created at DSR assignment for deals $10M+ GPV, and all deal-related communication should flow through that channel — not DMs. Here's how it works and what good looks like.

1
Deal Communication (Internal)
Slack-first, with full stakeholder visibility
💬A deal-specific Slack channel is automatically created at DSR assignment for $10M+ GPV deals. The Slack feed is visible directly from SFDC within the DSR record.
👥Add all deal stakeholders to the channel from day one — SAMPS, Sales leadership, Account Management, TAS, and any other relevant cross-functional partners.
📌PM will pin key resources to the channel — SOWs, project plans, call notes, and deal docs. Send all recaps and follow-ups here.
🔄The channel is updated automatically when DSR status changes. When the project is complete, Slack will prompt for the channel to be archived.
🚫All deal comms should be in the channel, not DMs. Visibility is critical for performance coaching, escalation handling, and continuity if team members change.
🔧When relevant, 3P resources can be added to the channel for real-time visibility and streamlined coordination.
2
Seller Communication (External)
PM-owned for the duration of the engagement
📅PM implements weekly syncs at project kick-off to discuss progress, blockers, and next steps. Weekly technical syncs with SolEng are added where applicable.
📧Email recaps follow every meeting — notes, progress updates, and action items. Email is preferred for cross-functional visibility, but PM will meet sellers where they're at (sometimes text or phone).
📣PM is also responsible for collecting, tracking, and filing feature requests for the duration of the project.
⚙️PM leverages tools like call transcripts, recordings, and automation to streamline and maintain communication quality at scale.

Project Closeout

What happens when implementation wraps up

A well-executed closeout is just as important as a strong start. The AU PM team drives the project to a clean conclusion — but we need input from the full deal team to do it well. Here's what happens as your deal moves into closeout.

✍️Seller acceptance is confirmed in writing. Before closeout is finalised, the AU PM team will obtain written confirmation from the seller that implementation is complete and accepted. This is required before any billing or handoff steps proceed.
💳Billing is triggered after seller acceptance. Once the seller has accepted in writing, implementation services are billed. The AE is responsible for ensuring the commercial side is in order — check with your PM rep if you have questions about timing or invoicing.
🔁An internal retro is held with the full deal team. The AU PM rep will facilitate a post-implementation retro involving the AE, AM, and any other key stakeholders. The goal is to capture lessons learned, document what went well, and surface areas for improvement on future deals. Your presence and candid input make it more valuable.
🤝The AU PM team facilitates the AM handoff. Once implementation is complete and billing is confirmed, the AU PM team manages the structured transition to the Account Manager — ensuring the AM has full context on the seller's setup, preferences, and any outstanding items. As the AE, you'll be asked to contribute context before the handoff is finalised.

How to File a DSR to Engage the AU PM Team

Engage early — Salesforce does the routing for you

There are no procedural requirements to submit a DSR for upmarket implementation. Engage the AU PM team as soon as the seller signals buying intent and "next steps" come into the picture. Salesforce will automatically route your DSR to a streamlined upmarket DSR form — no IDV, MT, SaaS, menu, or contract requirement upfront.

1
Submit your DSR in Salesforce — anytime after buying intent
From the Opportunity record, click "Deal Support Request". Salesforce will automatically route it to the upmarket DSR form. No need to select a team from a dropdown — the system handles it. Submit as early as you can; earlier engagement leads to better outcomes.
2
A Senior PM rep will connect with you to debrief
After assignment, a Senior AU PM rep will reach out directly to you — no ChiliPiper required. They'll debrief on the deal, ask clarifying questions, and begin solutioning. You don't need to wait for a formal kick-off meeting to start the conversation.
3
Scoping call — validate the solution and set expectations
A scoping call is scheduled with the seller (and you, the AE) to validate the proposed solution, surface risks, clarify what implementation will and won't deliver, and align on timelines. This call is the foundation of everything that follows — come prepared with your deal context.
4
Ensure your DSR is complete before handoff
Before the DSR is marked complete, it should contain the following:
  • Account region (AU, GB, EU)
  • Estimated contract value
  • Agreed SOW — signed or in progress
  • Rollout plan — locations, timeline, phasing
  • Proposed solution — what Square products/services are involved
  • What implementation will deliver — scope in
  • What implementation won't deliver — scope out
  • Deal Brief link
  • Retro doc link (if applicable)
💡 Deal Points: 1 point per DSA and 10 points at stage 6 / DSR completion. There is no maximum — keep filing.

How to Sell PM Services

How to position PM services when speaking with your sellers

Positioning AU PM team's services well at the right moment in the sales cycle can be the difference between a smooth implementation and a chaotic one — for you, for the seller, and for the AM who inherits the account. Implementation services have real value, and you should feel confident articulating that. Here's what you need to know to pitch the AU PM team effectively and get the commercial side right.

🎯
Use the AU PM Team Pitch Deck
There's a dedicated pitch deck for positioning PM services — find it in the Deal Docs & Tools section of the PM hub. Feel free to make a copy and customise it to your seller's specific needs, vertical, and use case. AU PM team can also help you tailor it before a key meeting.
📄
The AU PM team Builds a Custom SOW
Once you're ready to price implementation services, loop in your AU PM rep and they'll put together a bespoke Statement of Work (SOW) outlining the proposed services and pricing. The SOW is tailored to the seller — there's no standard package. See go/sow for the SOW template.
💬
Discounting Can Be Discussed Directly
Any discounting of implementation services can be discussed directly with your lead — no approval needed from PM leadership. There's flexibility in how services are packaged and priced based on the deal context.
⚠️
Always Charge Something
It's always preferable to charge something for implementation services rather than waiving the cost entirely. If full pricing isn't viable, consider adding the value of implementation into an ETF. Charging — even at a discount — signals the value of the work and sets better expectations with the seller.
