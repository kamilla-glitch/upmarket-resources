ESO's Approach to Implementation

The Enterprise Seller Operations (ESO) team is a full deal cycle resource — we're involved from pre-sale through to post-implementation to ensure every enterprise engagement is set up for success from the very start. We get involved early, before a deal is signed, to make sure discovery is thorough, solutioning is complete, and nothing is left to chance by the time a contract lands. Our remit covers high-complexity, first-party use cases as well as technical implementations that leverage the Square developer platform, APIs, or third-party integrations requiring deeper architectural input.

We lead on all Square for Franchises (SFF) deals, which demand a specialised approach given their multi-location, multi-stakeholder nature. For any deal requiring scoping support, solution validation, technical feasibility assessment, or early-stage project planning, ESO is the right team to loop in. We provide pre-sales deliverables — including sample project plans and Statements of Work — to give sellers and buyers clarity and confidence before a deal closes. We also work closely with AEs to help them position ESO services compellingly and win the right deals.

Our approach is built on warm handoffs at every stage, with bespoke services tailored to each seller's unique needs — there is no one-size-fits-all here. We operate on a shared ownership model with the AE/AM, giving everyone greater visibility, control, and fewer surprises throughout the project lifecycle. The result is a more consistent, high-quality experience for the seller, and a stronger foundation for long-term retention.

Sample Project Plans

Real-world project plans to use as starting templates for new enterprise deals

Project plans are living documents and the operational backbone of every implementation we run. They keep the deal on track, surface risks early, and give both internal teams and the seller a single source of truth on where things stand. The examples here span different verticals and deal structures — from large-scale entertainment rollouts to food service and healthcare chains — giving you a range of approaches to draw from. Use them as a starting foundation when spinning up a new plan, then adapt the structure, milestones, and workstreams to fit the specific scope, timeline, and stakeholder requirements of your deal. Always share a live version with your AE/AM so everyone is working from the same document throughout delivery.

3P Vendor Sample Docs

Installer playbooks, location checklists, and training sign-offs for third-party vendor engagements

When a deal involves a third-party vendor for hardware installation, staff training, or on-site support, the PM's role shifts slightly — you're coordinating across more parties, and documentation becomes even more critical. The Live Nation suite here is a strong model for any venue or multi-site deployment where a 3P installer is in scope. The installation playbook sets expectations for the vendor, the location checklist ensures nothing is missed at each site, and the training sign-off provides a formal record of completion. Use these as the baseline structure when onboarding any new 3P partner, and adapt them to match the specific scope and logistics of your engagement.

Square for Franchises

Kick-off templates and materials specifically for franchise-model enterprise clients

Square for Franchises is a distinct motion within our team — multi-location, multi-stakeholder, and often multi-market, with a layer of complexity that sets it apart from a standard enterprise deal. Franchisors and franchisees have different priorities, different levels of technical readiness, and often very different relationships with Square. The kick-off template here is purpose-built for SFF engagements and reflects that added complexity, covering franchisor alignment, franchisee onboarding sequencing, and the governance structure needed to manage rollouts at scale. Always start with this deck when kicking off an SFF deal, and make sure both the franchisor and relevant AE/AM are aligned on the plan before any work begins at the franchisee level.
